7 Solar Myths in India That Are Costing You Money

By Shree Vinayak Solar Agency · Updated January 2025 · 6 min read

Misinformation is one of the biggest reasons Indian homeowners delay switching to solar. Let's clear up the most common myths with real facts.

Myth #1

Myth

"Solar panels don't work well in winter or on cloudy days."

Fact

Solar panels generate electricity from daylight, not just direct heat. While output is somewhat lower on cloudy days, panels still produce 20-40% of their rated capacity. In Rajasthan especially, winters are typically clear and sunny, often producing excellent output.

Myth #2

Myth

"Solar maintenance is expensive and complicated."

Fact

Solar systems require minimal maintenance — mainly occasional cleaning, which costs little to nothing if done yourself. Annual professional check-ups typically cost a few hundred rupees, a tiny fraction of your total savings.

Myth #3

Myth

"Solar panels damage your roof."

Fact

When installed correctly by a qualified team, mounting structures are designed to protect your roof, not damage it. In fact, panels can shield your roof from direct sun and heat exposure, potentially extending its lifespan.

Myth #4

Myth

"The subsidy process is too complicated for an average person to handle."

Fact

While there is paperwork involved, a good installer manages the entire application, feasibility approval, and subsidy disbursement process on your behalf — you just need to provide basic documents.

Myth #5

Myth

"Solar panels only last a few years before they stop working."

Fact

Quality solar panels come with 25+ year performance warranties and typically continue producing 80%+ of their original output even after 25 years. Degradation is usually just 0.5-0.8% per year.

Myth #6

Myth

"You need to be rich to afford solar."

Fact

Between government subsidies (up to ₹78,000) and solar loan options with EMIs often lower than your existing electricity bill, solar is accessible to a much wider range of households than most people assume.

Myth #7

Myth

"Once installed, you're on your own if something goes wrong."

Fact

Reputable installers provide equipment warranties (typically 10-25 years on panels, 5-10 years on inverters) plus service support. Always confirm warranty and after-sales terms in writing before installation.
