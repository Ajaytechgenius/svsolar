PM Surya Ghar Yojana Explained: Eligibility, Subsidy & How to Apply

By Shree Vinayak Solar Agency · Updated January 2025 · 7 min read

PM Surya Ghar Muft Bijli Yojana is India's flagship rooftop solar scheme, aiming to bring free electricity to one crore households. If you're a homeowner considering solar, this scheme can cut your upfront cost dramatically. Here's how it actually works.

What Is PM Surya Ghar Yojana?

Launched by the Government of India, the scheme provides a direct subsidy to homeowners who install rooftop solar systems, aiming to make the first 300 units of electricity free for participating households every month.

Who Is Eligible?

  • You must be an Indian citizen owning a residential property with a suitable rooftop
  • The property should have a valid electricity connection in your name
  • You should not have availed a rooftop solar subsidy for the same property before
  • The installation must be done through an empanelled/registered vendor

Subsidy Amount by System Size

System SizeApprox. Central Subsidy
1 kW₹30,000
2 kW₹60,000
3 kW or above₹78,000 (capped)

Note: Subsidy amounts are set by the government and subject to periodic revision. Always confirm current figures before booking your installation.

Documents You'll Need

  • Aadhaar card
  • Latest electricity bill
  • Proof of property ownership
  • Bank account details (for subsidy disbursement)
  • Passport-size photograph

Step-by-Step Application Process

  • Step 1: Register on the official PM Surya Ghar portal with your state, discom, and electricity consumer number
  • Step 2: Submit your rooftop solar application with required documents
  • Step 3: Get your rooftop feasibility approved by the discom
  • Step 4: Choose a registered installer and get the system installed
  • Step 5: Submit plant details and apply for net-metering
  • Step 6: After inspection and commissioning certificate, submit bank details
  • Step 7: Subsidy is credited directly to your bank account within 30 days

📌 Pro tip: The subsidy is disbursed after installation is complete and verified — not before. Make sure your installer has experience with the paperwork so the process doesn't get delayed.

How Long Does the Whole Process Take?

From application to subsidy disbursement, most homeowners complete the entire process in 4-8 weeks, assuming documents are in order and the discom's feasibility approval isn't delayed.
