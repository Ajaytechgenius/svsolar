How Much Does a 3kW Solar System Cost in Rajasthan? (2025 Guide)

By Shree Vinayak Solar Agency · Updated January 2025 · 6 min read

If you're a homeowner in Sikar or anywhere in Rajasthan with a monthly electricity bill between ₹2,000–₹4,000, a 3kW rooftop solar system is usually the sweet spot. Here's exactly what it costs, what subsidy you'll get, and how fast it pays for itself.

3kW System Cost Breakdown

ComponentApprox. Cost (₹)
Solar panels (3kW)₹90,000 – ₹1,05,000
Inverter₹18,000 – ₹25,000
Mounting structure & wiring₹12,000 – ₹18,000
Installation labor₹8,000 – ₹12,000
Total (before subsidy)₹1,28,000 – ₹1,60,000

Government Subsidy Under PM Surya Ghar Yojana

For a 3kW system, eligible homeowners can receive a central government subsidy of approximately ₹78,000 (as per current PM Surya Ghar Muft Bijli Yojana slabs, subject to change — always confirm current rates before booking).

📌 Net cost after subsidy: Roughly ₹50,000 – ₹82,000 depending on your equipment choice and installer quote.

How Fast Does It Pay for Itself?

A well-installed 3kW system in Rajasthan (which gets excellent sun exposure most of the year) typically generates enough electricity to cover a ₹2,500–₹3,500 monthly bill almost entirely. Most homeowners recover their investment in 3.5 to 5 years, and the system continues producing free electricity for 20-25 years after that.

What Affects Your Final Price?

Three things move the number most: the brand of panels you choose (Indian vs. imported), whether you go with a string inverter or microinverters, and your roof's condition (older roofs sometimes need reinforcement, adding cost).

Real Numbers Beat Estimates

Every roof and bill is different — the fastest way to know your exact cost and subsidy amount is to run your numbers through a calculator or talk to an installer directly.
